Jo-El Sonnier is an American singer, songwriter and accordionist, country music performer and Cajun. Initially signing with Mercury Nashville Records, Sonnier put a number of small singles on the Billboard country charts in the late 1970s. In the late 1980s, he signed with RCA Records, breaking through with the Top 10 hits. “No More One More Time” and a cover of Richard Thompson's “Tear Stained Letter”. Although his chart success declined in the late 1980s, he continued to record music. He has recorded over 30 albums, mostly on independent labels.
