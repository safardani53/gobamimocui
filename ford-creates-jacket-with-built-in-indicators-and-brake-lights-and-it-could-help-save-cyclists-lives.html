Ford creates jacket with BUILT-IN indicators and brake lights - and it could help save cyclists' liv

May 2024 · 2 minute read

FORD has created a smart jacket fitted with its own indicators and brake lights - and it could make riding on the road safer for cyclists.

And the intelligent garment even connects to the rider's smartphone to tell them where to go.

Developed by some of Ford's employees with a keen interest in cycling, the jacket has sleeves that light up to show when the wearer plans on turning right or left.

It connects wirelessly to a navigation app on the rider's smartphone, which then vibrates the appropriate sleeve to tell the rider which way to go.

The tech means cyclists no longer have to worry about trying to navigate via their phone while concentrating on the road, and also makes it easier for other road users to know what their next move is.

The jacket even has a flashing brake light that can tell cars when the rider is slowing down.

It also links up to a pair of headphones to allow the rider to take phone calls, listen to music or hear navigation directs.

Ford's jacket could make road conditions safer for cyclists, who are often harder to see than other vehicles.

According to Cycling UK, some 1.9million Brits cycle every day, with a large number of these using their bike as a commuter vehicle.

And the most recent figures show there were almost 18,500 causalities involving cyclists in 2016, with just over 100 riders killed in the year.

But the new clothing technology could make it less risky for riders, but keeping them more focused on the road, and better alert drivers.

Tom Thompson, project lead for the Ford Smart Mobility team, said: “The smart jacket concept helps us to better understand how the different players that are a part of the urban mobility ecosystem – cyclists, cars, and pedestrians – can better co-exist through the application of smart technologies and how we can apply those learnings to future ideas.

“There is an immediate change in mind-set once there is no longer any need to stop to consult navigation apps directly on your phone – or worry if you’re heading into a particularly busy or dangerous road junction."

Ford are currently testing prototypes of the jacket in London.

The idea was brought to life by cycling clothing specialists, Lumo, and mobility software company Tome.
