Trendon Watford, an American NBA player, is a power forward for the Portland Trail Blazers (2021- now). The team picked him as an undrafted free agent in the 2021 NBA draft.

In the world of basketball, tireless effort & dedication is a testament to success, and Watford is no exception to that.

The Birmingham-born saw himself on a progressive path from being a highly touted high school prospect to a notable collegiate basketball player at LSU. Even more, he was the two times Alabama Mr. Basketball awardee.

Currently, Watford is on a four-year contract with the Blazers that sees him committed throughout the 2024-25 season.

In his rookie season (2021-22 season), he made his career-high 27 points against the Wizards.

Moreover, on March 5, 2022, Trendon scored his first-ever double-double with 12 points & 14 rebounds in a loss to the Timberwolves.

From his college basketball days at LSU to signing with the Blazers as an undrafted free agent, let’s delve into the story of Trendon Watford and how he overcame obstacles to pursue his dreams.

Details On Family & Early Life

Watford was born on November 9, 2000, in Birmingham, Alabama, as one of three children to Ernest and Belinda Watford.

Trendon, the youngest of all, has an elder brother Christian, a former basketball player, and a sister Elise (b. September 1996), a marketing coordinator.

The NBA player’s father is a retired sheriff deputy of Alabama’s Jefferson County, while his mother is a special needs teacher.

Trendon began playing basketball at four, but it turned into a passion only after he went to middle school.

His parents were their biggest supporters but also placed a high value on education.

Moreover, the Watford family has an emphasis on faith; as reported, the young NBA player always prays before his game.

Details On Siblings!

Elder Brother Played Basketball

Trendon Watford’s elder brother Christian Watford (b. April 28, 1991) is an American former basketball player. He played professionally from 2013 to 2019, primarily in the NBA G League teams.

An alumnus of Indiana University, Christian averaged 13.1 points & 5.9 rebounds per game during his four seasons as a collegiate basketball player.

After going undrafted in the 2013 NBA draft, Christian played for the Israeli team Hapoel Eilat for a season (2013-14).

Then, he would return to play in the G League with Maine Red Claws (2014-15), Reno Bighorns (2015), Fort Wayne Mad Ants (2016-17) & Raptors 905 (2017-19).

He works as a camp counselor for Tom Crean’s Basketball Camp (2009- now).

Talking about his personal life, the former player is now married & enjoys a family of three. He and his wife, Morgane Watford, married on July 15, 2017, after two years of engagement.

They share a daughter Macee Raine Watford who was born on April 26, 2020.

Education & Collegiate Career

Trendon Watford attended Mountain Brook High School in Mountain Brook, Alabama.

He led his team to four consecutive state championships at Brook and was named Mr. Basketball in Alabama twice. The player graduated from high school in 2019 with a 3.8 GPA.

Trendon then played college basketball for Lousiana State University (LSU) from 2019 to 2021, averaging 16.7 points, 7.4 rebounds, and 2.8 assists per game in his two seasons.

He was named to the All-SEC First Team and the All-SEC Tournament Team in 2021.  Trendon declared for the 2021 NBA draft but went undrafted.

NBA Career

After his sophomore year at LSU, Trendon Watford went for the 2021 NBA draft but was undrafted.

But it didn’t deter him, and he continued to pursue his dream of playing in the NBA as a roster for the Blazers in the 2021 NBA Summer League.

Watford’s NBA career began after the Portland Trail Blazers signed him on a two-way contract on August 3, 2021.

Later, in February 2022, he inked a four-year deal (Rest-of-Season) contract with the Blazers, joining notable players in the team: Eric Bledsoe, Joe Ingles, & Josh Hart, among others.

The NBA power forward made his NBA debut on November 14, 2021, against the Nuggets and his first-ever start in the rotation against the Timberwolves on March 7, 2022.

As a rookie, Watford made two double-doubles, scoring his first double-doubles in a loss against the Nuggets on March 5, 2022, scoring 12 points & 14 rebounds.

Moreover, Trendon scored his career-high 27 points on March 12, 2022, in a victory over Washington Wizards.

His rookie season (2021-22) averaged 7.6 points, 4.1 rebounds & 1. 7 assists per game.

In the 2022-23 season, the NBA player made his season-high 21 points against the Wizards on February 3, 2023.
