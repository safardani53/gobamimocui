Loose Women viewers slam Kelly Brook for saying drunk girls shouldn't get in taxis

July 2024 · 4 minute read

KELLY Brook left Loose Women viewers furious today when she said drunk girls shouldn't get in taxis and insisted they should always call a family member to take them home.

The 38-year-old model was discussing the release of serial rapist taxi driver John Worboys after just less than 10 years in prison, and revealed she didn’t understand why women got into cabs after they've been drinking.

She said: “Surely, if you just called a family member and said, ‘Come and get me, I can’t get home’, someone would come and pick you up.

“If you’re drunk, if you’ve had a few drinks, you’re so vulnerable, your guard is down, you just wanna get home, and I think a lot of young girls put themselves in that situation, and if I think if you are in that situation, just call somebody. Don’t get in a cab, don’t get on a train – you can get attacked on a train.”

Kaye Adams pointed out that Kelly’s brother is training to be a taxi driver –so has undergone strict background checks, and asked, “Wouldn’t you trust him?”

To which Kelly responded: “Well I wouldn’t go out and get silly drunk.”

“Well you have to trust people?” persisted Kaye, as Kelly said, “Well obviously not, no.”

Kelly went on to state that young women should just phone someone they can trust if they are “vulnerable” on their way home late at night.

She said: “I think there are a lot of abuser taxi drivers who have done this.

“You’re in a very vulnerable state, it’s late at night, it’s not just taxi drivers, it can be someone else on a night out, you are vulnerable.

“You are a young woman, it’s late at night, call someone you can trust and can give you advice on how to get home, I think that’s the best thing to do.”

Despite Kelly seemingly having good intentions and never actually blaming rape victims, viewers were furious with her standpoint.

One wrote: “@loosewomen Someone please educate Kelly Brook about rape myths, including drink, trust, vulnerability, reporting etc. #LooseWomen Thank goodness for Kaye Adams.”
