Rare 2021 Lincoln penny sells for $822 online - do you have one lying around?

September 2024 · 3 minute read

A PENNY minted last year that features first President Abraham Lincoln has sold for hundreds online.

While there is damage and missing features on the coin, it’s unclear if this is an error that happened through the mint.

Errors can include die errors, wrong planchets, and off-center errors. 

“Just to make things CLEAR I am in no way saying that this is a genuine error coin,” the seller wrote in a description. 

“I found this in a roll of coins from the bank that was in a collection I purchased. It could be a damaged coin or whatever.”

Based on the pictures provided, there is a line that runs vertically through the center of Lincoln’s head and almost through his entire upper body.

The element splits the abolitionist’s image in half. 

There is also a line that runs horizontally through Lincoln's nose to the back of his head. 

Moreover, most of the letters for “In God We Trust” are missing. 

The year 2021 is featured, with another date on the bottom of the obverse that is upside down.

Additionally, some damage and lines can be found on the reverse. 

While there was confusion by the seller, that didn’t stop users from engaging in a bidding war. 

The 2021-dated piece sold for a total of $821.59 after 31 bids on March 3.

That would mean the Lincoln cent is now worth more than 8.2million percent of its original denomination. 

Which Lincoln error coins are valuable?

There are a couple of Lincoln error pennies that could sell for thousands. 

One of them is the 1955 double die obverse penny.

A double die means that there is a duplicate of letters, numbers, or words on a coin.

The 1955 date is said to be the most famous one of them all.

It is worth $1,318 on average and up to $17,057 if in an MS63 grade, according to USA Coin Book.

There’s also a 1922 penny without a mintmark.

Typically, no mintmark pieces indicate that it was minted in Philadelphia – but not in this case.

This happened despite no single Lincoln penny from that year getting minted outside of Denver, meaning all should have a “D” mark.

However, sloppiness in the production process caused the mistake.

On average, the 1922 no letter mark piece is worth $970 and more than $30,000 if in an MS63 grade.

To see if your spare change is worth anything, you can check eBay by searching the full name, selecting the "sold" listing, and then toggling the search to "highest value.”

This will give you an idea of how much the coin might be worth, because your quarters half dollars, dimes, and nickels could be valuable too.
