Marc Leishman married wife Audrey Hills in 2010

For your information; Marc Leishman has been maintaining a successful personal life, just like his professional career.

Leishman is a married man. He got married to longtime girlfriend turned wife Audrey Hills on 24th April 2010. The pair exchanged the vows in an intimate wedding ceremony in front of their close friends and relatives.

Marc Leishman and wife Audrey Hills’ Children

Talking about Marc Leishman’s children, he is the father of three children. Many sites claim that he is the father of only two children, but Heavy reported that he shares three children (two sons and a daughter) with his wife. The name of his children is Oliver, Harvey, and Eva.

The family of five is leading a healthy familial relationship in Virginia Beach, Virginia.

Is Marc Leishman’s wife Audrey Dead?

Marc Leishman’s wife Audrey is alive and healthy at the moment. However, once, back on 31st March 2015, Audrey almost died suffering from toxic-shock syndrome; a rare and life-threatening complication of certain bacterial infections.

Audrey also suffered from an acute respiratory distress syndrome. She was induced into a coma which lasted for four days to pinpoint the illnesses.

During the tragic moment, Audrey’s last words to her husband were to look after their cats and to take their children to get their pictures with the Easter bunny.

CAPTION: Audrey Hills with her sons SOURCE: thegolfnewsnet

When Leishman heard of his wife falling ill, he rushed to be with her from Augusta National where he was practicing for the following week’s Masters.

The doctors only gave around 5% survival chance. But, Audrey’s medical condition got improved. However, the recovery is slow, and the journey is filled with pitfalls.

During an interview, Audrey claimed that it had been her worst nightmare. She said:

They went through their nightmare when I was in my coma, and when I woke up it was a big relief for them and that’s when my nightmare started.

Audrey battled with various respiratory infections for a year and a half. In 2016, at the Bay Hill, she was hospitalized after falling ill. She had to be pumped full of intravenous fluid and steroids.

In May 2015, Audrey underwent a tonsillectomy and spent most of her summer days downing antibiotics.

However, now, with proper treatment and medications, she is healthy at the moment.
