Leighton Bennett not kiddin' about before BDO worlds debut

January 2024 · 3 minute read

DARTS prodigy Leighton Bennett wants to stop being treated like a “freak show” and show age is no barrier in darts.

The 14-year-old has already won a senior BDO title and last June he beat the legendary Phil Taylor in a German exhibition.

Tonight, the reigning world youth champion will play 2015 Lakeside champion Scott Mitchell in the first round of the BDO World Darts Championship.

If the Lincoln lad beats Dorset farmer Mitchell at the Indigo nightclub, then maybe people will stop talking about his youth.

Bennett, the youngest male player in the tournament’s history, said: “I can’t wait to get to 16 years old and people stop talking about how old I am.

“I feel like a bit of a freak show. I want to grow up quickly and let people just talk about my ability and not my age.

“No one talks about what I’ve scored, my average or anything. It’s just that ‘he’s only a kid’.I know that’s just part of life and I do laugh about it.

“I probably get treated really nicely because of my age. But I just want to be an equal now really, judged on my ability.”

Taylor, 59, is a huge fan of Bennett and after their first meeting at the Tower of London, the 16-time world champion hailed him as the future of the sport.

Bennett is nicknamed Boom Boom and turned 14 on New Year's Eve. He has left full-time schooling and receives home tutoring thanks to financial support from agent Nevada Sports management and sponsors SAP and Target.

He added: “It’s a strange feeling when my mates are all going back to school this week and moaning about it.

“They think I’m really lucky. The truth is that I spend all my life practising and travelling to darts so I don’t get much time to myself.

“When they are spending hours on their PlayStation or Xbox, I’m at the dartboard trying to improve all the time.

“I’ve never even owned a video game of any sort.

“I know I’m lucky. SAP enable me to have a home tutor which allows me to work on my game and balance my life with schooling.

“That’s also a really difficult thing to do but it seems to be working for me right now.”

Naturally all the pressure will be on No9 seed Mitchell and this match could be the highlight of the doomed BDO world champs.

Cautious Mitchell, 49, told the Bournemouth Echo: “It’s an amazing achievement for him and he’s more than earned his place.

“He hasn’t been hand-picked, he hasn’t played a one-off game to get there, he’s played the full season and played that well for the full season.

“Leighton is the real deal, everybody knows that. I’m expecting people to want to be cheering Leighton and not wanting me to win. I get that.

“Everybody will have me down as the favourite but I’m not quite so sure. I think I could be the underdog for this one.

“I’m going to have to do special things to break him.”

Last night three-time Lakeside champion Martin Adams was beaten 3-2 by Scott Waites in the first round of his 26th world championships.

Adams had criticised BDO bosses in a SunSport interview, claiming they should have never left the Lakeside.
