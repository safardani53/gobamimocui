Kesha goes completely naked in a rushing river covered by only her latest album cover

May 2024 · 4 minute read

POP singer Kesha has posed nude in nature while tastefully placing an image of her album cover on her body.

Kesha, 36, took to Instagram on Monday with a new snap that showed her standing in the middle of a stream.

The Praying singer was perched in front of a rock formation with one leg bent as the water came up to her knees.

Kesha's side profile could be seen, showing the singer to be completely nude as she stood in the picturesque nature setting - really playing off her hit song title, Take It Off.

Her arm tattoos were in clear frame and her blonde, wet hair was slicked back as she looked off into the distance.

Although the 2010s phenomenon had her hand covering her chest, she added in an image of her album cover and tastefully placed it over her body to cover the side profile of her boobs.

LAWSUIT SETTLEMENT

Shortly after Kesha's album was released this year, the artist spoke out about her lawsuit settlement with music producer Dr. Luke as their years-long battle came to an end.

Both Kesha and Dr. Luke revealed via Instagram that a resolution had been agreed upon.

The agreement was settled following almost a decade of lawsuits and countersuits.

The case began in 2014 when Kesha publicly alleged that she was drugged and raped by the music producer nine years prior, while Dr. Luke alleged that she fabricated the claims to defame him.

The terms of the settlement were not released.

Both Kesha, a multiplatinum-selling singer, and Dr. Luke, a Grammy-nominated producer, released statements on social media.

“Only God knows what happened that night,” Kesha’s statement read.

“As I have always said, I cannot recount everything that happened. I am looking forward to closing the door on this chapter in my life and beginning a new one. I wish nothing but peace to all parties involved.”

Dr. Luke shared: "While I appreciate Kesha again acknowledging that she cannot recount what happened that night in 2005."

"I am absolutely certain that nothing happened.

“For the sake of my family, I have vigorously fought to clear my name for nearly 10 years. It is time for me to put this difficult matter behind me and move on with my life.”

The lawsuit had been scheduled to go to trial this summer.

During sworn questioning in 2017, Kesha said: “The abuse I suffered from Luke was a decade long, every day, every moment of every day."

Dr. Luke asserted that the claims were “completely untrue and deeply hurtful.”

He has not been charged with any crimes.

NEW SERIES

Kesha has been busy this year, as she also launched a new supernatural series on Discovery+ at the beginning of July.

Conjuring Kesha is a new series that shows Kesha and her famous friends exploring some of America's most notoriously haunted locations.

“To me, the supernatural comes naturally,” Kesha said in a press release, via Billboard.

"It started with my insatiable curiosity, my eternal searching for something bigger than myself. This has motivated my art, informed my music and has given a purpose to my entire life.

"It’s an eternal search for proof of God. But it’s the adventures that I have with my friends that take these pilgrimages to the next level and make them a reality."

She continued: "I wanted to catch actual proof of the unexplainable. If we could catch these things on camera, then what else could be true? My podcast, ‘Kesha and the Creepies,’ was the jumping-off point into the unknown – and now this show is the ultimate quest to go deeper into my existential questions. Trust me, you don’t wanna miss this.”
