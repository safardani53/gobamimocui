Brazil kit leaked ahead of World Cup... and new strip is a different shade of yellow

October 2024 · 3 minute read

BRAZIL'S kit for the World Cup appears to have been leaked - and it's a different shade of yellow.

The Samba stars go into Russia 2018 as one of the favourites and looking to become world champions for the sixth time.

Footy Headlines, who specialise in getting images of kits ahead of their launch, claim to have seen the home strip that Brazil will be wearing next summer.

According to the website, it follows the same template as England's new £110 Nike kit, with virtually no collar, and will be released with the away version in March.

Brazil rarely make big adjustments to their kit and there appears to be little difference between the new and current versions, aside from from the fact the new one is a little darker.

One thing that does seem clear, however, is the starting XI that will be wearing the kit - if they're fit.

Boss Tite told UOL Esporte he will stick with the same team which played in almost every South American qualifier over the past 18 months.

And he has named Gabriel Jesus - who recently returned to training with City - as the central forward in a three-man attack that also includes Paris Saint-Germain’s Neymar and Barcelona’s Philippe Coutinho.

Jesus is the sole representative from the Premier League despite his current absence from game time due to ligament damage.

But Tite appears to be confident that the 20-year-old will be 100 per cent fit in plenty of time for the start of the World Cup in June.

Roma’s Alisson Becker will be in goal, with PSG defenders Dani Alves and Marquinhos joined by Inter Milan’s Miranda and Real Madrid’s Marcelo.

Los Blancos team-mate Casemiro continues as Brazil’s anchorman, and will be supported in midfield by Barcelona’s Paulinho and Beijing Guoan’s Renato Augusto.
