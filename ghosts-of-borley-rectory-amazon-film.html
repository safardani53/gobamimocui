We go in search of The Ghosts Of Borley Rectory... and find Halloween hell

June 2024 · 3 minute read

IT is days before Halloween and I’m in one of our most haunted villages, in the dark, trying to pretend I am not terrified...without success.

Borley Rectory and its adjacent church in Essex have been plagued by tales of poltergeists, disturbing noises and terrifying apparitions of a crying nun since the 1800s.

Now Amazon horror film The Ghosts Of Borley Rectory tells the story of how paranormal researcher Harry Price investigated the strange goings-on in 1937.

He said he discovered the ghost of the nun, Marie Lairre, murdered and buried beneath the site of the rectory in the 17th century.

She has been seen or heard more than 300 times — and is still said to haunt the area.

The film’s director, Steven M Smith, told me: “I’ve visited Borley about 20 times and have always experienced something.

"One night I heard a woman crying behind me. I turned but nobody was there.”

Even paranormal enthusiast Michael Lirette warned me as I approached the village: “I once saw a cowled figure. It was the nun.

“The locals know she’s there but they don’t like talking about it. If you see her, don’t ridicule her — she’ll follow you home.”

Amazingly, that didn’t put me off.

I teamed up with ghost hunters Robert Garey and Adam Waughman, from Paranormal Essex, and medium Marie Winterbone to find the nun.

First stop was Borley Church, which sits on an eerily quiet country road with no lighting. By torchlight, we made our way past moss-covered tombstones towards the church.

“What was that?” I asked as I spun around to check who was behind me. I could have sworn I felt somebody touch my back. No one was there.

Something didn’t feel right so we left and headed to the haunted pub where investigator Harry stayed, Long Melford’s 15th-century Bull Hotel.

And it was here my Halloween hell began.

Built in 1450, as we walked along its creaky corridors, Adam suddenly stopped. “I’m not comfortable walking any further,” he said. “I can’t breathe. Something’s happened here.”

The hairs on my arms stood up as Marie revealed a murder took place on that very spot in 1648.

The 50-year-old told me: “Richard Everard was stabbed here. His spirit still haunts the hotel and guests often hear footsteps.”

Marie showed me a photograph taken during one of the paranormal evenings she hosts at the hotel.

'IT'S HAUNTED'

“We believe that’s the spirit of murderer Roger Greene,” she said.

She then took me to check out the hotel’s room four, saying: “It’s haunted by the spirit of an angry man.”

The atmosphere of the warm, welcoming room changed dramatically when Marie turned off the lights. Both she and Adam saw the silhouette of an old man perched on the edge of the bed.

Adam told me: “He is looking right at you.”

I was terrified and flicked the lights back on. Later, I questioned whether The Bull really was haunted...and that’s when I heard it: The awful sound of a woman’s pained wailing.

I asked night porter Paul Matthews, 66, if he had heard anything. He said: “No, but I do see shadows of people here.
