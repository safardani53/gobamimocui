Fury from residents and club members as local golf club is set to shoot dozens of geese for getting

August 2024 · 2 minute read

A GOLF club has been slammed over its plans to shoot dozens of birds.

Bosses say Canada Geese foul the course and get in the way of players’ shots.

A message to members says gunmen will mount an “aggressive campaign and try to shoot 3 or 4 on several evenings”.

The note adds: “Fingers crossed it works.”

Defra said a humane cull is permitted under licence, which Mr Alex Kerby said the pest teams had.

Alan Kerby, course secretary at West Essex Golf Club in Chingford, said the geese have visited for three years but this year has been “horrendous”.

He said: “Most golf courses like us do employ pest control fire arms experts.

"They have all the licences.”

Canada geese can be shot if they are damaging property - but under guidelines must be killed humanely.

Shocked members said the message didn’t give them confidence the humane guideline would be met.

One blasted: “I certainly don’t want these birds killed in the name of golf.

“I love the sport - but it’s not that important. This is just a seasonal problem.
