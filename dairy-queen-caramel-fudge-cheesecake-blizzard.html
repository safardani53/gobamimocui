Dairy Queen brings back fan-favorite blizzard and customers are begging to keep all year round

September 2024 · 3 minute read

DAIRY Queen has brought back a popular blizzard flavor and fans are begging it to stay on the menu for a little longer.

The fast food chain first launched the caramel fudge cheesecake blizzard as part of its menu last summer.

Now it's back for another summer.

The treat features soft serve ice cream that's mixed with cheesecake and salted caramel pieces, as well as decadent fudge.

"This indulgent treat is the perfect end to a summer day, meant to be enjoyed on a warm night as you watch a summer sunset blaze across the sky," Dairy Queen said in a description of the caramel fudge cheesecake blizzard.

But most importantly, the treat has become a fan-favorite since first becoming available last summer.

One fan begged Dairy Queen on Twitter to "keep the caramel fudge cheesecake year round."

Others were just happy to see it back as one commenter said: "Yay im gonna get one."

The caramel fudge cheesecake blizzard is available to order at participating Dairy Queens in mini, small, medium, and large sizes.

While prices can vary by store, the cost ranges anywhere from $4.46 to $6.50 depending on the size, according to a local menu viewed by The U.S. Sun.

The item will reportedly be available only for a limited time.

The U.S. Sun has reached out to Dairy Queen for comment.

MORE DAIRY QUEEN NEWS

But the caramel fudge cheesecake blizzard addition is far from the only menu change that Dairy Queen has made recently.

Another includes the new cake batter cookie dough item.

It features confetti cookie dough pieces and sprinkles, as well as a mix of both cake batter and Dairy Queen's traditional soft serve ice cream.

The new item has had fans seeing heaven over it.

Other new blizzards Dairy Queen has launched include the Oreo brookie and peanut butter puppy chow flavor.

When it comes to drinks, Dairy Queen has launched the summertime sunset.

It is an ice blend that features mango and strawberry flavors.

To save money, make sure you sign up for the fast food chain's loyalty program, DQ Rewards.

There, you can review offers under the "deals" section.

For example, Dairy Queen is offering a free soft drink after purchasing a six-piece chicken strip basket for a limited time.

SUMMER OF FAST FOOD

In addition, other fast food chains have been updating their menus for the summer.

For example, The Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf has launched new budget offerings for the summer.

This includes a breakfast bundle, as well as a $4 and a $3 menu.

The coffee chain also debuted two new menu items: the aloha cream cold brew coffee and peach cobbler cold brew tea.

Both will be available to order through August 15.

McDonald's fans have spotted the cookies & crème pie in the US.

While prices can vary by location, the sweet treat contains 220 calories and sells for $1.99.

McDonald's told The U.S. Sun that it will only be available in participating markets including Los Angeles, California; Seattle, Washington; Chicago, Illinois and New York, New York.

Tim Horton's has also released four new drinks that start at $3.50.

This includes the creamy coconut iced capp, white chocolate macadamia flavored drinks, and two beverages with a watermelon twist.

Tim Horton's is also allowing guests to buy an eligible cold drink and get another one for 50 percent off during its digital happy hour.
