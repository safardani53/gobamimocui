Terence Blanchard (Trumpet Player) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

May 2024 · 3 minute read

Terence Oliver Blanchard is an American trumpeter and composer. He began his career in 1982 as a member of the Lionel Hampton Orchestra, then followed by The Jazz Messengers. He has composed over forty soundtracks and performed over fifty copies. A frequent collaborator with director Spike Lee, he was nominated for two Academy Awards for composing the music for Lee's BlacKkKlansman and Da 5 Bloods films. He won five Grammy Awards out of fourteen nominations. From 2000 to 2011, Blanchard was the artistic director of the Thelonious Monk Institute of Jazz. In 2011, he was appointed artistic director of the Henry Mancini Institute at the University of Miami. In the fall of 2015, he was appointed Visiting Scholar in Jazz Composition at Berklee College of Music. In 2019, the University of California, Los Angeles named Blanchard as their preferred president of Jazz Research, where he will remain until 2024.
