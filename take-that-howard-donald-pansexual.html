Take That's Howard Donald says he is open-minded about being pansexual after listening to Christine

July 2024 · 7 minute read

IF you thought Howard Donald had Said It All, think again.

The Take That star, who has been married to Katie Halil, the mum of his two kids, for four years, says he is open-minded about being pansexual.

He told his fans he had started to think differently about his sexuality after listening to French singer Christine and the Queens.

Howard said during an Instagram live video: “Christine And The Queens, she’s a pansexual, I’ve read.

“Pansexual sounds good. Maybe I should be pansexual.

“And let’s face it, women look at other women sometimes and think, ‘Ooh yeah, she’s tasty, I fancy her’.

“I’ve looked at men before and thought, ‘Oh yeah, I fancy him, he’s a good-looking guy. I wish I looked like that’.

“You can still be attracted to men and still be attracted to women...as well as women, sorry. But I’m happy with my wife, anyway.

“But I’m just saying, I’m very open-minded when it comes to that.”

Enjoy explaining that one when you get home to your missus, Howard.

He is back on the road from April 12 with Take That bandmates Gary Barlow and Mark Owen.

They will play a series of dates on their Greatest Hits tour around the UK and Dublin.

It’s still not been confirmed whether they will be joined by Jason Orange who quit in 2014.

The lads had hoped he would take part, but Howard admitted that Jason had gone off the grid and was ignoring the band’s phone calls and emails.

Robbie Williams has promised he will return, saying he would join the band at some point during the UK leg of the tour.

He said: “As long as we stay healthy, I’m sure there’ll be another moment where we all get together. Maybe we can coax Jason back, who knows?”

I think they just need to have a little Patience.

    Rami on 'sex rap' director

    RAMI MALEK has said working with director Bryan Singer on Queen biopic Bohemian Rhapsody was “not pleasant”.

    The actor spoke out after Singer had a number of sexual misconduct allegations levelled against him in America.

    Rami, who played Freddie Mercury in the hit movie, told The Hollywood Reporter: “In my situation with Bryan, it was not pleasant, not at all. And that’s about what I can say about it at this point.

    “I’ve sat here and talked about how everyone deserves a voice and anyone who wants to talk about what happened with Bryan deserves to have their voice heard.

    “My heart goes out to anyone who has to live through anything like what I’ve heard and what is out there.”

    Singer has denied the accusations made against him by four men, some of which involve claims of underage sex.

    Rami said he was “not aware” of the accusations when he signed up to the Oscar-nominated film.

    Singer was sacked from Bohemian Rhapsody for failing to turn up to work just weeks before filming ended.
