Snoop Dogg slams Oprah for smiling and laughing with rapist Harvey Weinstein after bashi

April 2024 · 3 minute read

SNOOP Dogg has slammed Oprah Winfrey in a scathing Instagram post right after bashing Gayle King for her recent interview about Kobe Bryant's past.

He accused the media mogul of "tarnishing" Michael Jackson's name - and "smiling and laughing" with "rapist" Harvey Weinstein years before sexual assault allegations brought him down.

The rapper ranted that Oprah "did that fake a** Michael Jackson s**t to tarnish his name," adding: "Here she is with a known rapist smiling and laughing."

"F**k u and Gayle. Free Bill Cosby," he said.

Snoop was seemingly referring to Oprah’s interview last year with the late King of Pop's alleged sexual abuse victims, Wade Robson and James Safechuck, following the HBO documentary Leaving Neverland.

Though Oprah once associated with Harvey, she later said on Gwyneth Paltrow's podcast:  "What I knew about Harvey was that Harvey was a bully and that if Harvey's on the phone, you go 'God, you don't want to take the call,' because you're going to get bullied in some way."

Weinstein is currently standing trial on a string of rape and sexual assault charges.

Snoop's attack on Oprah follows him bashing CBS This Morning host King for asking about Kobe Bryant’s 2003 sexual assault allegations in an interview with former WNBA star Lisa Leslie.

Charges against Kobe were eventually dismissed, and a civil lawsuit was settled out of court.

The outspoken star recorded a video on social media saying: “What do you gain from that? I swear to God, we the worst, we the f***ing worst."

He continued: "We expect more from you, Gayle, don’t you hang out with Oprah? Why you all attacking us, we your people.

"You ain’t coming after f***ing Harvey Weinstein asking him dumb-a** questions. I get sick of you all…

“I wanna call you one … Funky, dog-haired b***h, how dare you try and tarnish my motherf***ing homeboy’s reputation, punk motherf***er.

“Respect the family and back off, b***h, before we come get you.”

During Gayle's interview she asked Lisa: “It has been said that his legacy is complicated because of the sexual assault charge which was dismissed in 2003, 2004.

"Is it complicated for you as a woman, a WNBA player?”

Lisa responded: “It’s not complicated for me at all.”

“I just have never seen him as being the kind of person that would do something violating to a woman or be aggressive in that way. That’s just not the person I know.”
