Pop megastar launching comeback this month and planning first arena tour in six years

August 2024 · 7 minute read

KYLIE MINOGUE will be giving herself a huge workload as a birthday present this year when she launches a comeback just days before turning 55.

I can reveal the Aussie singer is to unveil the lead single from her 15th studio album later this month, ahead of her big day on May 28.

She has worked on the track with top producer Lost Boy, who has previously worked his magic on hits for Ellie Goulding, Becky Hill and Mimi Webb. 

The album will follow this autumn, with a major arena tour being planned for 2024, her first in six years.

My music insider revealed: “Kylie has finally finished the album and is now raring to go with her return to music.

“It is being kept under lock and key and hardly anyone at her label has even heard it.

"The first single will be revealed later this month.

“It’s classic Kylie and is sure to go down well with her fans.”
I told in February how fans would have to wait longer for a new album after Kylie decided to write more songs for it,

It will be her first since 2020’s Disco, which beat Little Mix to No1 and became the fastest-selling record by a solo act in the UK that year.

Over the past few weeks, speculation has been rife online that Kylie would follow in the footsteps of Madonna and perform as an interval act at the Eurovision Song Contest in Liverpool next weekend, after Madge did so at Tel Aviv in 2019.

SINGLE RYLAN’S FOREIGN POLICY

EUROVISION could be the perfect place for Rylan Clark to find a boyfriend – because he needs to meet a foreigner who doesn’t know him.

The Radio 2 host, who is in Liverpool to head the station’s song contest coverage, said lots of potential suitors are intimidated by his celebrity status.

Rylan explained: “The biggest issue for me now is I’ve been single for two years, and of course I’d love to meet someone.

"I want the dream of the husband and the kids.

“I have said for so long that I need someone Italian, Swedish or Spanish.

“I don’t care who it is, just somebody who hasn’t got a clue.

"I want to meet someone the old-fashioned way.”

Rylan, who split from his husband Dan Neal in 2021, had a taste of that last year when he pretended to be someone else for an evening.

He told the How To Fail podcast: “I went to Barcelona last year on my own and for an entire night, this group of people thought my name was Josh and I was a lawyer, and it was f***ing great.

“It was amazing until right at the end of the night someone came up to me and went, ‘Oh my God, Rylan, can I have a photo?’.

"It blew my cover.

“If you can make me laugh and you’re decent, I don’t care what they look like, hand on heart.

"My type is gone, because my type doesn’t work for me, clearly. I just want someone nice.”

GEORGE MICHAEL will be posthumously inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall Of Fame.

It was revealed yesterday as the 13-strong list of stars was announced in the US.

Fellow British great Kate Bush will also receive the honour, following the renewed success last year of her hit Running Up That Hill.

She has had unsuccessful nominations three times previously.

Other artists in there include Missy Elliott, Willie Nelson, Rage Against The Machine, Sheryl Crow, Chaka Khan and Sir Elton John’s songwriting partner Bernie Taupin.

The induction ceremony takes place at New York’s Barclays Center on November 3.

ED SERIES SUMTHING BEAUTIFUL

ED SHEERAN feared his four-part Disney+ series The Sum Of It All would be “a propaganda piece” if he edited it himself.

He gave bosses freedom to use the footage as they wanted, warning it’s an “uncomfortable but beautiful” watch.

Ed attended the premiere, held at the Times Center in New York on Tuesday ahead of yesterday’s release, wearing a black jacket with matching trousers and a white T-shirt.

He said: “I had loads of footage.

“I didn’t want it to be a propaganda piece.

“So I was like, ‘Here is a load of footage and you guys decide what it is and just follow me’.

“They have made what I think is a really beautiful documentary. It is uncomfortable for me at times to watch but I think people will connect to it.”

Ed, whose new album Subtract – his sixth studio record – is released tomorrow, added: “The series tackles subjects that everyone goes through.

“So it’s not like, ‘I’m a famous person, these are famous problems’.”

THERE is no stopping Tom Grennan, who is back with yet another party anthem in time for the summer.

Last night the singer dropped his latest single, How Does It Feel, as Radio 1’s Hottest Record, after debuting it during a sell-out arena tour in March.

Tom said: “This song is a reflection of a moment in my life, when I realised I had let something unreal slip.

“It’s a reflection on the excitement that comes when you know you have a chance to rebuild.

“It’s exciting, fun and gives you the energy to want to get up and do what you believe in.”

His 14-track album,What Ifs & Maybes, is out on June 16 and features the song.

Tom, said: “For this whole album, I’ve definitely made a conscious decision for it to be a record people can just dance to.

“An album where people can listen in their own time, or fans can come to a show and they can have that sense of escapism.

“I want them to feel they have no stress and no worries when they’re listening to these songs.

“So I made that conscious decision to write some up-tempo songs that can live in a party world.

“How Does It Feel massively does that.”

NICKI MINAJ has things all tied up in her new video with Kim Petra.
